<p><p>In the healthcare and medical system integrators market, horizontal integration involves the merging of organizations at the same level within the healthcare system, such as hospitals consolidating or forming networks to enhance service offerings. In contrast, vertical integration focuses on connecting different stages of healthcare delivery, like combining hospitals with outpatient facilities or pharmacies, to streamline operations and improve care coordination. Both types enhance efficiency, reduce costs, and ultimately aim to provide better patient outcomes through cohesive systems.</p></p>
