<p><p>The fluid chillers market is primarily segmented into water-cooled and air-cooled systems. Water-cooled chillers utilize water as a heat exchange medium, making them efficient for large-scale applications, often in industrial settings. They require cooling towers and have a higher initial investment but lower operating costs. In contrast, air-cooled chillers use ambient air to dissipate heat, making them easier to install and maintain, ideal for smaller applications or areas with limited space. Each type serves distinct cooling needs and operational environments.</p></p>
