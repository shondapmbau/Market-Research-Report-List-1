<p><p>The Fire Emergency Lamp market is categorized into two main types: centralized control and non-centralized control. Centralized control types are managed from a single control panel, allowing for streamlined monitoring and activation of emergency lighting across a facility. In contrast, non-centralized control types operate independently, relying on individual units that activate automatically during emergencies. This differentiation caters to various needs in safety, installation complexity, and response efficiency, with centralized systems often preferred for larger infrastructures, while non-centralized options suit smaller setups.</p></p>
