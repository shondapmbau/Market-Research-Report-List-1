<p><p>The Lyme disease vaccine market primarily consists of two types: inactivated and attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines are made from killed pathogens and stimulate an immune response without causing the disease, offering safety and stability. Attenuated vaccines, on the other hand, contain live pathogens weakened to prevent disease while eliciting a robust immune response. Both types aim to prevent Lyme disease by promoting immunity, but their production methods and safety profiles differ, influencing market preferences and healthcare strategies.</p></p>
