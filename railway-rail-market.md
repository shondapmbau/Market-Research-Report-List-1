<p><p>Railway rail markets are categorized into heavy rail and light rail systems, each serving distinct purposes. Heavy rail, typically characterized by high-capacity trains, operates on exclusive tracks, catering to urban transit and long-distance travel with greater speeds and larger passenger volumes. In contrast, light rail systems are designed for shorter distances, operating on shared or dedicated tracks with lower capacity and speed. These systems often connect neighborhoods to city centers, enhancing accessibility and reducing traffic congestion in urban areas.</p></p>
