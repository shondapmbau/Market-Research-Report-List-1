<p><ul><li>Long (bast) Fibers</li><li>Short (core) Fibers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hemp products market is divided into two main fiber categories: long (bast) fibers and short (core) fibers. Long fibers, extracted from the outer stalk, are strong and versatile, making them suitable for textiles, ropes, and biodegradable composites. Short fibers, obtained from the inner core, are softer and primarily used in insulation, paper, and animal bedding. Both types cater to growing eco-friendly industries, emphasizing sustainability and the demand for natural materials in various applications.</p></p>
