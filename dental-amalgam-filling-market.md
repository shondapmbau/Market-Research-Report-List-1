<p><ul><li>Low-copper Amalgam</li><li>High Copper Amalgam</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental amalgam filling market is divided into two main types: low-copper amalgam and high-copper amalgam. Low-copper amalgam, containing about 5% to 6% copper, offers basic properties but is more prone to corrosion and marginal deterioration. In contrast, high-copper amalgam, with over 12% copper, provides improved strength, reduced corrosion, and better aesthetics, making it the preferred choice for modern dental practices. Both types are widely used for restorative dentistry, offering durability and effectiveness in filling cavities.</p></p>
