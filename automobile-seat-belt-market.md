<p><p>The automobile seat belt market comprises various types designed for different safety needs. Two-point seat belts secure the occupant at the waist, while three-point seat belts add a shoulder strap for enhanced protection. Four- and five-point systems, often used in racing and specialized vehicles, offer additional restraint across the body to minimize movement during a crash. Six-point seat belts provide maximum security by anchoring at the shoulders and hips. Other seat belt types include specialized configurations for unique vehicle designs or passenger requirements.</p></p>
