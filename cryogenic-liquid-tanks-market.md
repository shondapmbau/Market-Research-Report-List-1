<p><p>The cryogenic liquid tanks market is primarily categorized into two types: Vacuum Powder Insulation Type and Atmospheric Pressure Powder Insulation Type. Vacuum Powder Insulation Type tanks utilize a vacuum layer between inner and outer shells, minimizing heat transfer and preserving cryogenic liquids at extremely low temperatures. In contrast, Atmospheric Pressure Powder Insulation Type tanks feature powdered insulation at atmospheric pressure, offering a less efficient but cost-effective option for storing cryogenic fluids. Both types cater to various industrial applications, including LNG and industrial gas storage.</p></p>
