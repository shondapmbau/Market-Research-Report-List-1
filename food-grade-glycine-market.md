<p><p>The food grade glycine market can be segmented into three main types: Strecker synthesis, hydantoin hydrolysis, and biosynthesis. </p><p>Strecker synthesis involves the reaction of ammonia and formaldehyde with glyoxylic acid, producing glycine as a byproduct. Hydantoin hydrolysis converts hydantoin compounds into glycine through hydrolysis processes. Biosynthesis utilizes microorganisms or enzymes to produce glycine naturally, offering a sustainable approach. Each method provides varying purity levels and production efficiencies, catering to diverse applications in food and beverage, dietary supplements, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
