<p><ul><li>Stereolithogrphy Apparatus (SLA)</li><li>Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Three Dimension Printing (3DP)</li><li>Fused Depostion Modeling (FDM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rapid prototyping in the automotive market involves several key technologies that facilitate the quick production of prototypes for design verification and testing. Stereolithography Apparatus (SLA) uses a laser to cure liquid resin into solid parts, enabling high detail and smooth finishes. Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM) layers adhesive-coated paper to create prototypes. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses a laser to fuse powder materials for strong prototypes. Three-Dimensional Printing (3DP) creates objects layer by layer from various materials. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes thermoplastic filament to build prototypes, offering versatility and ease of use. Each method serves distinct needs in the automotive design process.</p></p>
