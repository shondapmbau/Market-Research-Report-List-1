<p><p>The fused alumina market comprises two primary types: white fused alumina and brown fused alumina. White fused alumina is characterized by its high purity and hardness, making it ideal for applications in ceramics, abrasives, and refractory materials. It is favored for its whiteness and low iron content. In contrast, brown fused alumina is produced from bauxite and has higher toughness and grit, commonly used in grinding wheels, abrasive blasting, and in various metallurgy applications. Both serve essential roles in industrial processes.</p></p>
