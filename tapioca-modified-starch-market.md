<p><p>The tapioca modified starch market is categorized into three main types: physical modification, chemical modification, and resistant starch. Physical modification involves processes like heat and moisture treatment to enhance texture and stability. Chemical modification uses reagents to alter starch properties for improved viscosity and solubility. Resistant starch, on the other hand, is designed to resist digestion, providing health benefits such as improved gut health and lower glycemic response. Each type serves diverse applications across food, beverages, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
