<p><p>The medical radiation shielding market is divided into two main types: full body protection and partial protection. Full body protection involves comprehensive shielding garments or barriers that cover the entire body, ensuring minimal exposure to harmful radiation during medical procedures. In contrast, partial protection focuses on shielding specific body parts, such as the abdomen or chest, providing targeted safety while allowing for flexibility and ease of movement. Both types aim to enhance staff and patient safety in environments with radiation exposure.</p></p>
