<p><p>The kosher foods market comprises three main types: Kosher Pareve, Kosher Meat, and Kosher Dairy. Kosher Pareve foods are neutral, meaning they contain neither meat nor dairy, allowing them to be consumed with either group. Kosher Meat products must adhere to specific slaughtering and processing laws, ensuring they are fit for consumption according to Jewish dietary laws. Kosher Dairy products include milk, cheese, and eggs, which must come from kosher animals and be processed using kosher equipment. Each category caters to diverse dietary needs within the Jewish community.</p></p>
